## *$364 million buys a lot of silence*

In January, a Navajo man was detained by Immigration and Customs Enforcement officers for hours. He had his tribal citizenship card. He had his birth certificate—it didn't matter. They held him anyway. 

In Washington state, ICE agents stopped actress Elaine Miles—Shoshone-Bannock, known for her roles on *Northern Exposure* and *The Last of Us*—and asked her if she was Mexican.

In Minneapolis, four members of the Oglala Sioux Tribe were swept up during an immigration raid. Their tribal president called DHS. He called the Bureau of Indian Affairs. A month later, he still had no information—not their names, not where they were held, not whether they'd been released. **The federal government simply swallowed four Indigenous people and went silent.**

This is what the Department of Homeland Security has done to Indians under the leadership of Kristi Noem over the past 14 months. She's now out of a job, and the Trump Administration has tapped Oklahoma Sen. Markwayne Mullin—a Cherokee citizen—to fill her spot. Surely this has been met with widespread backlash from the tribe, right?

On Thursday, Cherokee Nation Principal Chief Chuck Hoskin Jr. released a statement congratulating Mullin on his nomination to lead it. He called the appointment "historic." He said it was "deeply encouraging" to have someone with "a keen understanding of federal Indian policy, law and justice" elevated to such a critical role. He called Mullin's nomination "a testament to the resilience, capability, and enduring impact of our people."

Oh. How nice of you, Chuck. *You really shouldn't have.*

Let me get this straight. The Principal Chief of the Cherokee Nation—the largest tribe in the country—just congratulated a fellow citizen for being nominated to lead the agency that is racially profiling, detaining, and terrorizing Indians across the country. How tone-deaf, even by CN Communications Department standards.

### Level with me Chuck. Are you fucking stupid?

Tribal leaders supporting Sen. Mullin would be contentious even before this whole debacle. Mullin has a shaky record of supporting tribal sovereignty and wellbeing of his fellow citizens. In 2013 he voted against reauthorization of the Violence Against Women Act, a bill that was to include landmark protections for LGBT and Native American victims. By the time it was signed into law by then-President Barack Obama it only included stripped down protections for those vulnerable groups, a direct attack on tribal jurisdiction.

In the current decade, Mullin is a staunch supporter of the Trump Administration's policies, including legislation like the "Big Beautiful Bill," which included $46 billion for a border wall and funding for 10,000 new ICE agents. All the while Medicaid cuts are hitting Indian Country especially hard. According to data from the Kaiser Family Foundation over 150,000 Native Oklahomans rely on the program for access to healthcare, yet he repeatedly votes against funding and expansion that directly benefits his constituents and fellow Cherokees.

I will acknowledge that not everything Mullin has done is bad. In 2018, he helped to strip colonial-era blood quantum requirements from the Stigler Act, and last year co-authored the Tribal Warrant Fairness Act which allows U.S. Marshals to help tribal law enforcement find missing children and arrest suspects with warrants. The problem herein lies that Markwayne Mullin is yet another white-passing politician using Cherokee identity when convenient and dropping it when the party asks.

This comes to a point when you realize this isn't naivety on Hoskin's part. It's strategic.

### So that's where the money comes from...

Cherokee Federal, the federal contracting arm of Cherokee Nation Businesses, explicitly lists immigration and homeland security as core markets. According to the Cherokee Phoenix, it had scored over $5 billion in contract wins since 2012 and represented nearly 42% of CNB's total revenue at the time of reporting in 2020.

In 2021, it received a $706 million sole-source contract for processing unaccompanied migrant children. Last year it hired a "Senior Director of Homeland Security Solutions" to *expand* the DHS portfolio. Hoskin himself testified before Congress that Cherokee Federal contributes $364 million to tribal services—and said he was "worried" about anything threatening that revenue.

Hoskin isn't just congratulating the new DHS secretary. He's congratulating the new boss of Cherokee Federal's biggest growth market. The "warmest congratulations" are a business development strategy wearing tribal pride as a costume. Sovereignty that doubles as a revenue model for federal contracting isn't sovereignty—it's a business arrangement. 

While Cherokee Nation expands its DHS contract portfolio and rolls out congratulations, the Oglala Sioux have banned ICE from their lands. So have the Rosebud Sioux, the Chippewa Cree, and the Ute Indian Tribe. They chose their people over access. Hoskin chose a revenue stream.

Cherokee Federal reported $364 million flowing back into tribal services last year. That's the price of the Chief's silence on what DHS does to Indians. Now you know what it costs.
